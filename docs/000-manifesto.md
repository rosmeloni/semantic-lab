# Manifesto

> Business meaning is one of the most valuable assets an organisation possesses. Yet it is rarely managed as one.

## Why semantic-lab exists

Organisations invest heavily in collecting, storing and processing data. They build data warehouses, analytics platforms, AI assistants and automation systems.

However, one fundamental challenge remains largely unresolved:

**Business meaning is fragmented.**

Concepts such as *Customer*, *Revenue*, *Active User* or *Churn* are often defined differently across departments, applications, reports and documentation. As organisations evolve, these differences accumulate, creating ambiguity, inconsistent decision making and increasing governance costs.

The emergence of AI makes this challenge even more significant.

Large Language Models are increasingly capable of analysing data, generating code, answering business questions and automating workflows. However, AI cannot reliably infer what a business concept should mean. It can only reason from the information it is given.

Without a shared understanding of business semantics, AI risks amplifying inconsistencies rather than reducing them.

---

# Our belief

We believe that business meaning should become a first-class enterprise asset.

Business concepts should be:

- explicitly defined
- version controlled
- governed
- traceable
- understandable by both humans and machines

Just as source code is collaboratively developed through modern software engineering practices, enterprise semantics should evolve through transparent governance supported by automation.

---

# The role of AI

Semantic-lab does not seek to replace human judgement.

Business meaning belongs to people.

Humans define business truth.

AI assists by:

- detecting inconsistencies
- analysing downstream impact
- identifying semantic drift
- maintaining documentation
- validating implementations
- proposing improvements

AI proposes.

Humans approve.

---

# Guiding principles

## Business meaning is owned by humans

Business definitions are organisational decisions, not machine-generated facts.

---

## Semantics should be executable

Business concepts should not remain static documentation.

They should be represented in a way that allows systems, analytics platforms and AI agents to reason over them consistently.

---

## Governance should enable innovation

Governance should not become bureaucracy.

The goal is to reduce ambiguity while allowing organisations to evolve quickly and safely.

---

## Every semantic change has consequences

Changing the definition of a business concept may affect reports, KPIs, applications, APIs, documentation and AI systems.

Understanding those impacts should become easier, not harder.

---

## AI should augment, not replace

Automation should remove mechanical work.

Human expertise should remain responsible for business intent.

---

# What semantic-lab is

Semantic-lab is an open research project exploring how humans and specialised AI agents can collaboratively build, maintain and evolve enterprise business semantics.

The project combines ideas from:

- semantic modelling
- knowledge representation
- data governance
- software engineering
- AI agents
- product thinking

Its purpose is not to produce definitive answers, but to investigate better ways of representing business meaning in the age of AI.

---

# A long-term vision

Imagine an organisation where:

- every business concept has one approved definition
- every change is versioned and traceable
- AI understands enterprise terminology consistently
- semantic inconsistencies are detected automatically
- business and technical teams collaborate through a shared semantic language

This is the future that semantic-lab seeks to explore.
